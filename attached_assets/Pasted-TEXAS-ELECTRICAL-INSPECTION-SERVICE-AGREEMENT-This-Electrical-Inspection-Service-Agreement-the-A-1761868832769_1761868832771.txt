1. SCOPE OF SERVICES
1.1 General Scope
The Contractor agrees to perform comprehensive electrical safety inspections (the "Services"
or "Inspections") for the residential units and common areas of the Property as specified in
Exhibit A (Schedule of Services). All Services shall be performed in accordance with:
● The 2023 National Electrical Code (NFPA 70) as adopted by the State of Texas
effective September 1, 2023, pursuant to Texas Occupations Code §1305.101
● NFPA 70B-2023 (Standard for Electrical Maintenance) best practices
● NFPA 70E (Standard for Electrical Safety in the Workplace)
● ANSI/NETA MTS-2023 (Maintenance Testing Specifications)
● Applicable local amendments and municipal electrical codes for [City/Municipality]
● Industry standards and practices recognized by the Texas Department of Licensing and
Regulation (TDLR)
1.2 Per-Unit Inspection - Detailed Scope
For each residential unit specified in Exhibit A, the Contractor shall perform the following
inspections of accessible and visible electrical components:
A. Electrical Panel Assessment
1. Infrared Thermography Scan:
○ Thermal imaging of all accessible electrical panels, breakers, and connections
○ Equipment shall be inspected under at least 40% of nominal load conditions
where feasible
○ Temperature differentials exceeding 15°C (27°F) between similar components
under similar loads shall be documented
○ Thermal and visible light images shall be captured and included in the report
2. Physical Panel Inspection:
○ Visual inspection for physical damage, corrosion, moisture intrusion, burn marks,
or overheating indicators
○ Verification of proper panel mounting and security
○ Check for adequate working clearance (minimum 36 inches per NEC 110.26)
○ Inspection of panel location for code compliance (not in bathrooms, closets, or
areas exposed to physical damage)
○ Verification of proper grounding and bonding connections
○ Check for proper panel labeling and circuit identification
○ Inspection for double-tapping, improper wire connections, or undersized wiring
○ Verification that wire connections are secure and properly torqued
○ Check for unsealed openings or missing knockout plugs
3. Circuit Breaker and Overcurrent Protection:
○ Verification of proper circuit breaker sizing and type for connected loads
○ Testing of main breaker operation (where safe and feasible)
○ Visual inspection for signs of arcing, overheating, or failure
○ Verification of proper amperage ratings
○ Check for recalled or defective breaker brands
B. Service Entrance and Main Disconnect
● Inspection of service entrance conductors for damage or deterioration (where
accessible)
● Verification of proper service rating (minimum 150 amps per Texas Minimum
Construction Standards for multi-family dwellings)
● Check for proper clearances from windows, roofs, and structures
● Verification of proper grounding electrode system connection
C. In-Unit Electrical Devices and Wiring
1. Receptacle Testing and Inspection:
○ Testing of all accessible outlets for proper grounding, polarity, and functionality
○ Verification of GFCI protection in required locations per NEC 210.8(A):
■ Bathrooms
■ Kitchens (countertop receptacles)
■ Garages
■ Outdoor receptacles
■ Basements and unfinished areas
■ Laundry areas
○ GFCI testing using approved test equipment
○ Check for two-prong (ungrounded) outlets
○ Inspection for damaged, loose, or improperly installed receptacles
○ Verification of proper outlet spacing per NEC requirements
2. AFCI Protection Verification:
○ Verification of Arc-Fault Circuit Interrupter protection in required areas per NEC
210.12:
■ Bedrooms
■ Living rooms, family rooms, dining rooms, libraries, dens, sunrooms
■ Closets, hallways, laundry areas (unless exempted)
○ AFCI testing where accessible
3. Lighting Fixtures and Switches:
○ Inspection of all accessible light fixtures for proper installation and condition
○ Testing of light switches for functionality
○ Check for proper fixture support and securing
○ Inspection for overheating, discoloration, or damage
○ Verification of proper bulb wattage ratings
4. Visible Wiring:
○ Inspection of all accessible and visible wiring for:
■ Proper insulation and condition
■ Signs of damage, wear, or deterioration
■ Proper securing and support per NEC requirements
■ Evidence of amateur or improper modifications
■ Knob-and-tube or aluminum wiring (where visible)
■ Extension cord abuse or permanent use of temporary wiring
5. Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Detectors:
○ Verification of smoke detector presence and functionality per Texas Property
Code §92.251
○ Testing of smoke detectors (where accessible without causing unit disturbance)
○ Verification of carbon monoxide detector presence where required
○ Check for proper placement and mounting
D. Permanently Connected Appliances
● Visual inspection of permanently wired appliances (where accessible):
○ Water heaters
○ HVAC equipment electrical connections
○ Dishwashers
○ Garbage disposals
○ Built-in microwaves
○ Range/oven connections
● Verification of proper disconnect means
● Check for proper bonding and grounding
E. Special Conditions and Safety Hazards
● Investigation and documentation of any:
○ Shock hazards
○ Fire hazards
○ Electrical surge indicators
○ Evidence of water infiltration or moisture damage
○ Improper modifications or DIY electrical work
○ Code violations observed during inspection
○ Inadequate electrical capacity for unit demand
1.3 Common Areas Inspection - Detailed Scope
For common areas specified in Exhibit A, the Contractor shall perform the following inspections:
A. Main Distribution and Service Equipment
1. Main Service Equipment:
○ Infrared thermography of main distribution panels, switchgear, and bus
connections
○ Visual and mechanical inspection of all accessible components
○ Verification of proper service capacity for building demand
○ Check for proper grounding and bonding systems
○ Inspection of main disconnect and overcurrent protection
○ Verification of proper clearances and access per NEC 110.26
2. Sub-Panels and Distribution Panels:
○ Infrared and visual inspection of all common area distribution panels
○ Verification that house loads (common area loads) are not supplied from
individual dwelling unit panels per NEC 210.25(b)
○ Verification of separate "house panel" for shared and common area loads
○ Check for proper panel labeling with unit numbers served
○ Inspection of panel location and accessibility
○ Verification of proper grounding and bonding to main panel
B. Common Area Lighting Systems
1. General Lighting:
○ Inspection of all common area lighting fixtures (lobbies, hallways, stairways,
parking areas)
○ Verification of adequate illumination levels (4 footcandles for exterior pedestrian
pathways per Dallas Municipal Code §27-11, where applicable)
○ Testing of lighting functionality
○ Check for proper fixture mounting and security
2. Emergency Lighting and Exit Signs:
○ Verification of emergency lighting in required locations per NFPA 101:
■ Buildings more than three stories in height
■ Interior corridors and stairways
■ Exit access and exit discharge areas
■ Doors with delayed-egress locks
○ Testing of emergency lighting systems:
■ Minimum 90-minute duration capacity
■ Average illumination of 1 footcandle (0.1 footcandle minimum) along
egress paths
■ Activation within 10 seconds of power failure
○ Verification of monthly testing records (if maintained by property)
○ Inspection of illuminated exit signs for functionality and compliance
3. Exterior Lighting:
○ Inspection of building entrance lighting
○ Parking area lighting assessment
○ Verification of GFCI protection for exterior receptacles
○ Check for proper weatherproof enclosures
C. Fire Alarm System Integration Points
● Visual inspection of fire alarm panel electrical connections (where accessible)
● Verification of proper integration with electrical systems
● Check for proper backup power supply
● Documentation of any observed deficiencies (Note: Comprehensive fire alarm testing is
outside the scope of this Agreement unless specifically contracted)
D. Elevators and Mechanical Rooms
● Inspection of elevator electrical disconnect and emergency power (where accessible)
● Visual inspection of mechanical room electrical panels and equipment
● Verification of proper clearances and access
● Check for environmental hazards (moisture, corrosion, etc.)
E. Parking Garages and Exterior Areas
● Inspection of parking garage electrical systems (where present)
● Verification of proper GFCI protection
● Check for damaged or deteriorating equipment
● Inspection of outdoor electrical equipment and enclosures
1.4 Testing Equipment and Calibration
All testing equipment used in the performance of the Services shall be:
● Properly calibrated within the previous twelve (12) months in accordance with NETA
MTS-2023 standards
● Appropriate for the specific measurements and tests being performed
● Used in accordance with manufacturer specifications
Calibration records shall be maintained by the Contractor and made available upon request.
1.5 Comprehensive Reporting
Upon completion of each inspection cycle, the Contractor shall provide the Client with a
comprehensive written report (the "Report") containing:
1. Executive Summary:
○ Overview of inspection scope and methodology
○ Summary of critical findings
○ Priority recommendations
2. Detailed Findings for Each Unit:
○ Unit identification
○ List of all components inspected
○ Findings categorized by severity:
■ Critical (C1 - Danger Present): Immediate safety hazards requiring
emergency action
■ Urgent (C2 - Potentially Dangerous): Significant deficiencies requiring
prompt attention
■ Advisory (C3 - Improvement Recommended): Non-urgent
recommendations for improvement
○ Photographic documentation (including IR images where applicable)
○ Specific code violations identified (with NEC references)
3. Common Areas Summary:
○ Consolidated findings for all common area systems
○ Infrastructure assessment
○ Priority repair recommendations
4. Compliance Assessment:
○ Summary of NEC 2023 compliance status
○ Local code compliance (to the extent observable)
○ Required upgrades or modifications
5. Recommendations:
○ Clear, prioritized action items
○ Suggested remediation timelines based on severity
○ Cost implications (general estimates, if requested)
6. Documentation and Disclaimers:
○ Inspector name, credentials, and license number
○ Date and conditions of inspection
○ Equipment used and calibration status
○ Limitations and exclusions (as detailed in Section 3)
○ Required legal disclaimers

All Services shall be performed by:

● Underground wiring or services

● Load calculations or electrical demand studies (unless specifically contracted)
● Power quality analysis or harmonic distortion testing
● Electromagnetic field (EMF) measurements
● Short-circuit or coordination studies (unless specifically contracted)

E. Environmental and Specialized Hazards:
● Presence of environmental hazards including but not limited to:
○ Asbestos
○ Lead paint
○ Toxic or contaminated materials
○ Mold, mildew, or fungus
○ Radon or other airborne hazards
○ Underground storage tanks

● Pest, rodent, or insect infestation

● Generator systems (standby or portable)
● Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) systems
● Battery backup systems
● Electric vehicle charging stations (unless specifically contracted)

H. Operational and Performance Testing:
● Systems that are shut down, disconnected, or non-operational
● Systems that do not respond to normal operating controls
● Efficiency testing of electrical equipment
● BTU, capacity, or performance ratings
● Operational testing requiring specialist tools or knowledge
● Testing that would require interruption of essential services
I. Code Compliance Certification:
● Comprehensive code compliance inspection (beyond observable defects)
● Municipal permit verification or permitting services
● Final inspection for occupancy permits (unless specifically contracted as municipal
inspector)
● Building code compliance unrelated to electrical systems
● ADA compliance review
● Energy code compliance analysis

9. FAILED INSPECTIONS AND REMEDIATION
PROTOCOLS
9.1 Definition of Failed Inspection
An inspection is considered "failed" if the Report identifies one or more of the following:
(a) Critical (C1) deficiencies: Immediate safety hazards presenting danger of shock,
electrocution, or fire, including but not limited to:
● Exposed energized conductors
● Missing or damaged panel covers
● Severe overheating detected via thermography (>40°C delta-T)
● Main grounding electrode system failure
● Severely overloaded circuits
● Active arcing or signs of electrical fire
(b) Urgent (C2) deficiencies: Potentially dangerous conditions requiring prompt attention,
including but not limited to:
● Missing GFCI protection in required locations
● Missing AFCI protection in required bedrooms
● Improperly sized circuit breakers
● Evidence of amateur electrical work
● Double-tapping in panels
● Significant code violations
(c) Multiple Advisory (C3) deficiencies: While individual C3 items are non-urgent
recommendations, if a unit or common area has more than ten (10) separate C3 deficiencies,
it may be considered a failed inspection requiring comprehensive remediation plan.
9.2 Client's Remediation Obligations
Upon receipt of a Report indicating failed inspection(s), the Client shall:
(a) Immediate Action Required (C1 Deficiencies):
● Take emergency corrective action immediately upon notification (within 24 hours)
● For life-safety issues, the Contractor will notify the Client by phone/email immediately
upon discovery
● Hire licensed electrical contractors to perform emergency repairs
● Do not delay for cost estimates or bidding processes when safety is at issue
(b) Urgent Action Required (C2 Deficiencies):
● Initiate corrective action within seven (7) days of receiving the Report
● Complete corrective action within thirty (30) days of receiving the Report
● Obtain all necessary permits as required by local jurisdiction
(c) Advisory Items (C3 Deficiencies):
● Address recommendations within a reasonable timeframe (suggested 90 days)
● Prioritize C3 items that, while not immediately dangerous, could lead to future failures
● Document planned remediation schedule
9.3 Standard Remediation Timeline
Except as specified in Section 9.2 for C1 and C2 deficiencies, the Client shall complete all
remediation work and notify the Contractor within:
● Emergency repairs (C1): Immediate to 48 hours
● Critical repairs (C2): 30 days
● Standard repairs (C3): 90 days (recommended)
These timelines align with Texas Property Code §92.052 which establishes a rebuttable
presumption that seven (7) days is a reasonable time for repairs affecting physical health or
safety (though electrical emergencies may require faster action).
9.4 Extension of Remediation Period
The Client may request an extension of remediation deadlines by providing written notice to the
Contractor explaining:
● The specific reason for delay (contractor availability, permit delays, parts on back-order,
etc.)
● The proposed new completion date
● Interim safety measures being taken
The Contractor shall not unreasonably withhold approval of reasonable extension requests,
particularly if:
● The delay is due to circumstances beyond the Client's control
● Temporary safety measures are in place
● The Client is making diligent good-faith efforts to complete repairs
However, C1 (immediate danger) deficiencies may not be delayed without implementing
adequate temporary safety measures (such as de-energizing affected circuits, posting warnings,
or restricting access).
9.5 Re-Inspection Procedures
(a) Scheduling Re-Inspection:
Upon completion of all required remediation work, the Client shall:
● Notify the Contractor in writing that repairs are complete
● Provide copies of any permits or inspection approvals from licensed electrical
contractors
● Provide documentation of work performed (invoices, permits, completion certificates)
● Request scheduling of re-inspection
(b) Re-Inspection Timing:
The Contractor shall perform the re-inspection within:
● C1 (emergency) deficiencies: Within 48 hours of Client's request (if feasible)
● C2 (urgent) deficiencies: Within seven (7) business days of Client's request
● C3 (advisory) deficiencies: Within fourteen (14) business days of Client's request
(c) Re-Inspection Scope:
The re-inspection shall be limited to verification that the previously identified deficiencies have
been corrected. The Contractor is not required to perform a complete re-inspection of all
systems unless separately contracted.
(d) Re-Inspection Fees:
● First Re-Inspection: $250.00 per unit or $500.00 per common area floor (as
applicable)
● Second and Subsequent Re-Inspections: $350.00 per unit or $750.00 per common
area floor
The first re-inspection fee is waived IF:
● All remediation is completed within the prescribed timeline (Sections 9.2-9.3), AND
● The re-inspection confirms all deficiencies have been properly corrected, AND
● No new deficiencies are discovered that should have been addressed during
remediation
(e) Multiple Trip Fee:
If the Contractor must make multiple trips to the Property because remediation work is
incomplete or inadequate, each additional trip shall be charged at $350.00 per affected unit or
$750.00 per affected common area floor, plus any travel/mobilization costs.
9.6 Repeated Failed Inspections
If any unit or common area fails re-inspection twice (meaning the remediation work was

(a) The Contractor may require the Client to engage a licensed Master Electrician (different

(b) The third and subsequent re-inspections shall be charged at $500.00 per unit or $1,000.00

(c) If a pattern of repeated failures emerges (more than 25% of units requiring three or more

● Recommend a comprehensive electrical system audit by a professional engineer
● Exercise its right to terminate this Agreement per Section 10 if the Client's repeated

11. RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL FOR ELECTRICAL
SERVICES
11.1 Purpose and Scope
To ensure the ongoing quality, safety, and integrity of the Property's electrical infrastructure, and
to maintain the validity and continuity of the Contractor's inspection-related insurance coverage,
the parties agree to the provisions of this Section 11.
11.2 Right of First Refusal
(a) Notice Requirement:
If the Client receives a bona fide written offer from a licensed third-party electrical contractor
to perform any electrical work at the Property (other than emergency repairs requiring
immediate attention), including but not limited to:
● Routine electrical maintenance or repairs
● Electrical system upgrades or modifications
● Remediation of deficiencies identified in the Contractor's Reports
● New electrical installations or expansions
● General electrical contracting work
The Client shall provide the Contractor with written notice containing all material terms of such
third-party offer within five (5) business days of receiving it.
(b) Exercise Period:
The Contractor shall have fifteen (15) business days from receipt of the Client's notice to
notify the Client in writing of the Contractor's election to perform such electrical work on the
same terms and conditions as set forth in the third-party offer.
(c) Acceptance:
If the Contractor timely exercises its right of first refusal by matching the third-party offer, the
Client shall engage the Contractor to perform the work on the terms specified in the third-party
offer. The parties shall execute a separate work order or service agreement documenting the
specific scope, timeline, and any additional terms.
(d) Client May Proceed with Third Party:
If the Contractor does not exercise its right of first refusal within the fifteen-day period, the
Client is free to engage the third-party contractor on the terms specified in the original offer.
11.3 Exceptions to Right of First Refusal
The right of first refusal in Section 11.2 does not apply to:
(a) Bona fide emergencies where immediate repair is necessary to prevent injury, property
damage, or code violations, and waiting 15 business days would create unreasonable risk.
However, the Client shall notify the Contractor of such emergency work as soon as practicable.
(b) Tenant repairs that are the tenant's responsibility under their lease agreement and
performed at tenant's expense (not the Property owner's or HOA's expense).
(c) Utility company work performed by the electric utility provider for service connections,
meter installations, or utility-owned equipment.
(d) Municipal requirement inspections performed by city/county electrical inspectors for
permitting or code enforcement (as opposed to private inspection services).
(e) Work performed by contractors already under contract to the Client as of the Effective
Date of this Agreement, for projects that were bid, awarded, and documented prior to this
Agreement (grandfather clause). However, renewals or extensions of such contracts after the
Effective Date are subject to the right of first refusal.
11.4 Approved Third-Party Contractors
(a) If the Client elects not to engage the Contractor for electrical work (either because the
Contractor declined to match the third-party offer or the exception in Section 11.3 applies), and if
such work is related to remediation of deficiencies identified in the Contractor's Reports,
the Client shall notify the Contractor of the selected third-party contractor's name, license
number, and scope of work.
(b) The Contractor reserves the right to review and approve the qualifications of the third-party
contractor to ensure:
● Valid Texas electrical contractor license
● Appropriate insurance coverage (at least statutory minimums)
● No history of major code violations or license suspensions
● Qualified personnel for the scope of work
(c) The Contractor's approval shall not be unreasonably withheld but may be conditioned on
the third-party contractor meeting equivalent professional standards. Approval or disapproval
shall be provided within ten (10) business days of the Client's notification.
(d) Effect of Disapproval:
If the Contractor reasonably disapproves a third-party contractor (with written explanation of the
basis), the Client may:
● Select a different contractor subject to the same approval process, or
● Engage the Contractor to perform the work (if the Contractor is willing and able), or
● Proceed with the disapproved contractor at the Client's own risk, acknowledging that:
○ The Contractor's insurance coverage may not extend to issues arising from such
work
○ The Contractor may require additional documentation or re-inspection before
resuming Services
○ Future Reports may include disclaimers about work performed by unapproved
contractors
11.5 Effect of Unapproved Work on Agreement
If the Client engages a third-party contractor to perform electrical work without complying with
Sections 11.2 or 11.4 (i.e., fails to provide notice and opportunity to match, or proceeds with
unapproved contractor for remediation work):
(a) The Contractor may:
● Require photographic documentation and certification of the completed work before
performing future inspections
● Require a comprehensive re-inspection of affected systems at the Client's expense
● Add specific disclaimers to future Reports regarding areas worked on by unapproved
contractors
● In cases of repeated violations, terminate this Agreement per Section 10.2 (material
breach)
(b) The Contractor's insurance coverage and warranties may not extend to:
● Defects or issues arising from work performed by unapproved contractors
● Interactions between systems inspected by the Contractor and modifications made by
unapproved contractors
● Any claims alleging the Contractor "should have known" about defects introduced by
unapproved work performed without the Contractor's knowledge
11.6 Expiration of Right
This Right of First Refusal shall:
● Commence on the Effective Date
● Continue throughout the Initial Term and any Renewal Terms
● Terminate automatically upon expiration or termination of this Agreement
● Not extend beyond the term of this Agreement or encumber the Property
11.7 No Unreasonable Restraint of Trade
This Section 11 is intended to:
● Protect the Contractor's legitimate business interest in maintaining the integrity of the
electrical systems it inspects
● Ensure continuity of electrical maintenance standards
● Prevent conflicting work that could affect the reliability of the Contractor's Reports
It is not intended to unreasonably restrain trade or prevent the Client from engaging qualified

● Epidemics, pandemics, or public health emergencies
● War, invasion, terrorism, or armed conflict
● Strikes, labor disputes, or lockouts (not involving the party's own employees)
● Riots, civil unrest, or insurrection

● Inability to obtain necessary materials, supplies, or equipment
● Cyberattacks, hacking, or technology failures beyond the party's control
17.2 Notice and Mitigation
(a) Notice:
A party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:
● Notify the other party within seven (7) days of becoming aware of the event
● Describe the nature of the Force Majeure Event and its expected impact
● Provide reasonable updates as circumstances evolve
(b) Mitigation:
The affected party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to:
● Minimize the impact of the Force Majeure Event
● Resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable
● Find alternative means of performance if feasible
17.3 Suspension of Performance
During a Force Majeure Event:
(a) The affected party's performance obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the
Force Majeure Event, but only to the extent performance is actually prevented or materially
hindered by the event.
(b) The non-affected party's payment obligations for Services already performed shall not be
suspended.
(c) Deadlines and timeframes shall be extended by the duration of the Force Majeure Event,
provided the affected party is diligently working to resume performance.
17.4 Termination for Extended Force Majeure
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and
substantially prevents either party from performing its material obligations:
(a) Either party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the other
party;
(b) Upon such termination:
● The Client shall pay for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the
termination date
● Neither party shall be liable to the other for consequential damages resulting from the
Force Majeure termination
● All other termination provisions in Section 10.5 shall apply
17.5 Force Majeure Does Not Excuse Payment
Force Majeure does not excuse the Client's obligation to pay for Services already
performed prior to or during the Force Majeure Event, nor does it excuse payment of
undisputed invoices that became due before the Force Majeure Event occurred.

(a) If federal, state, or local authorities impose restrictions that prevent in-person inspections
